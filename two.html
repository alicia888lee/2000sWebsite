What the Internet Looked Like in 2000

Introduction

If you tried getting on the internet around the year 2000, you would hear the famous sound of a 56k dial-up connection connecting. It was the soundtrack of an era before fast broadband and Wi-Fi!

The Internet's Baby Steps

In 2000, the internet was still figuring itself out. Websites looked blocky, slow to load, and often garish. CSS wasn't a thing yet and HTML ruled the web like a giant, pixelated world of color and clunky graphics.

The Birth of Google

At the turn of the century, Google was just starting out. People were still using Yahoo! to search for things. But little did they know, Google was going to change everything, and that search bar would soon become the key to the entire world.

The Dancing Baby

In the year 2000, the internet wasn’t just a tool for email and research. It was also a place for viral content—like the Dancing Baby. Before YouTube and TikTok, there was this odd little dancing 3D baby that became an internet legend.

Instant Messaging Craze

For millions of kids, the internet meant Instant Messaging. Whether it was AIM or MSN Messenger, typing to friends in real-time was the ultimate way to communicate. You’d often hear the sound of a new message popping up while you were chatting. The best part? Custom emoticons and colorful text!

The End of the 56k Era

Eventually, we said goodbye to dial-up internet. A new age of broadband connections arrived, and suddenly, webpages loaded faster, downloads were quicker, and we didn’t have to share the phone line anymore.

Conclusion

As the years went on, the internet changed drastically. But let’s take a moment to remember how it all started, with slow connections, pixelated graphics, and wild designs. The internet in 2000 was a time capsule of what was to come—a place where kids like us first learned what it meant to be online.
